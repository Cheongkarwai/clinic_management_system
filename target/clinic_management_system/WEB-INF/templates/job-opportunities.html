Job Opportunities

Registered Nurse

Primary responsibilities include caring for sick, injured, or recuperating patients and interacting with patients and family. They also evaluate patients, do tests, keep patient records, and assist physicians during operations. Registered nurses educate patients about their problems and provide treatment recommendations. They may also provide them emotional assistance.

Pharmacists

Primary responsibilities include preparing and dispensing prescription prescriptions as well as educating patients on how to take their medications properly. They understand what drugs treat, how they interact with other medications, and how to prescribe them correctly. In addition, pharmacists monitor pharmacy inventory and interact with hospital doctors regarding specific medications. They may provide wellness checkups, provide health advice, and administer immunizations to patients.

Surgeon

Surgeons perform surgical procedures on people to mend injuries or treat illnesses or disorders. They often focus on one form of surgery, such as orthopaedic, reconstructive, or paediatric surgery. Surgeons also diagnose and treat patients before and after surgery, provide medicines, and maintain patient records. During procedures, they collaborate closely with nurses and anesthesiologists.

Medical Admission Clerk

Admissions clerks meet patients as they enter the hospital and collect their medical and personal information, as well as the purpose for their visit. They check the patient in and enter their information into the hospital's computer system. They also gather and enter insurance data. Medical admissions clerks may answer phones, collect data, and communicate with nurses and physicians while they are not engaging with patients.

Medical Records Clerk

Medical records clerks' primary responsibilities include organizing and filing patient information such as personal and medical histories, as well as admission and discharge documentation. They provide hospital departments with the documents and paperwork that they require. They are in charge of keeping records safe, secret, and up to date. During hospital audits, medical records clerks also supply information and assistance.

Coding Specialists

Coding experts work mostly in the billing department. They classify and code health claims so that the hospital may be reimbursed by insurance companies and government health-care programs. They analyze claims to ensure they are eligible for reimbursement and utilize computer tools to assign the appropriate codes to procedures. In addition, they help with insurance companies to address disputed claims.
